# NERI Staking & Voting

:::warning
**NERI token is not yet live or available. Be very diligent of scams. Go slow. 🐌** 
:::

Guide to staking NERI tokens and participating in governance.

## What is NERI?

NERI is the native governance token of the Nerite protocol. NERI holders are members of the **Go Slow Foundation DAO LLC**, a legally recognized entity incorporated in the Marshall Islands. This means NERI holders have real, legally recognized governance rights — not just onchain signaling.

### No Separate Entities

Unlike most DeFi protocols, Nerite has **no separate DAO, foundation, operational company, or labs entity** extracting value. There is no Nerite Foundation, no Nerite Labs, no opco taking a cut. It's just NERI. The token itself is the entire governance and value-flow structure. This is core to Nerite's governance-minimized design.

### What NERI Governs

NERI governs the flow of **25% of all fees** earned by the Nerite protocol. Specifically, holders have the power to:

1. **Direct 25% of protocol revenue** — interest payments earned by the protocol flow to initiatives and liquidity directed by NERI holders.
2. **Update collateral debt limits** — increase or decrease the debt limits for each collateral type the system accepts. Setting a debt limit to 0 effectively shuts down that collateral branch, allowing only repayments.
3. **Control the shared treasury** — vote on how treasury funds are allocated.
4. **Delegate ARB** — ARB deposited in the protocol can be delegated by NERI governance.

### What NERI Cannot Do

Nerite is governance-minimized. NERI holders do **NOT** have the power to:

- Mint new USND or control user Troves.
- Upgrade the protocol or change its rules.
- Add new collateral types.
- Change the fee split or behavior of protocol interest payment fees.
- Change any core protocol parameters.

The protocol's smart contracts are immutable. Governance exists only where it is strictly necessary.
